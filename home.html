About the Event

The KIN Graduate Research Day brings together students across research and applied pathways within the School of Kinesiology — including Neuromechanical, Socio-Cultural, Psychological, Biological and Physiological Studies, and the Master of Kinesiology (MKin) program.

In our shared space within the Gateway Health Building, this day creates opportunities for dialogue across methodological, theoretical, and applied approaches that may not regularly intersect in day-to-day lab or program life.

I
Interdisciplinary Exchange
Dialogue across neuromechanical, socio-cultural, psychological, and physiological approaches to kinesiology research.
II
Presentation Experience
Graduate students develop research communication skills through 3-Minute Thesis, oral, and poster presentations.
III
Community Building
A shared space to connect with peers, faculty, and collaborators across the full breadth of the School of Kinesiology.

Land Acknowledgement
Land Acknowledgement
The Kinesiology Graduate Student Society respectfully acknowledges that the Kinesiology Graduate Research Day is hosted on the traditional, ancestral, and unceded territory of the xʷməθkʷəy̓əm (Musqueam) People. We are grateful to the Musqueam people for their enduring stewardship of these lands and waters, and we recognize the responsibility we share in supporting and respecting Indigenous communities — past, present, and future.

As members of the School of Kinesiology, we recognize that sport, movement, health, and physical activity have been shaped by colonial histories and frameworks that continue to influence academic knowledge production. We are committed to reflecting on whose bodies, voices, and ways of knowing are centered within our research and to fostering spaces that respect and learn from Indigenous histories, lands, and communities.
